About Me

Originally from Faridabad, Delhi NCR, I began my academic journey studying History at the University of Delhi — where I led the Women Development Cell and headed the Editorial Board. That grounding in critical thinking and institutional history shaped everything that came after.

"Policy without people is just paper. I build the bridge between the two."

After years of preparing for India's UPSC Civil Services Examination and working as a volunteer educator at Prakashdeep, I pivoted to international public policy study at King's College London — bringing lived experience of governance challenges in the Global South into conversation with cutting-edge policy frameworks.

Today I work at the intersection of participatory democracy, digital governance, and public sector transformation. My consultancy work with Hackney Council's Corporate Transformation Team has given me hands-on delivery experience in local government reform.
